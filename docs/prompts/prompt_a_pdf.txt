Most term plans mention "rider" only in this boilerplate and have zero actual compatible riders. Only list a rider if the document names a SPECIFIC rider by its UIN (e.g. "512B210V02"). Empty compatible_riders is a valid, expected, common result — not a missed extraction.
2. Tax language: Do not extract tax-benefit boilerplate ("consult your tax advisor", Section 80C/10(10D)) into any field. Identical across all documents, zero discriminative value.
3. Nullable fields are real, not failures: sum_assured_max may legitimately be "No Limit, subject to underwriting" — encode as null with a note, never fabricate a number. age_at_maturity_min/max may be genuinely absent for some plans — encode null if not present, do not guess.
4. Two fields are flagged NOT YET VERIFIED across most of this category — extract if present, set confidence "low", and do not treat absence as an error:
